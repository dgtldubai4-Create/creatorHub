A woman with a camera
sees the room differently.

I started with events — the champagne blur of Dubai galas, conferences, weddings — because events are where people forget the camera exists. That's where honest photographs live.

The same instinct travels: to fashion weeks where the best frame is backstage, not on the runway; to sports where a race is decided in a two-thousandth of a second; to portraits where the real face appears only after the "official" one.

“Anyone can photograph what a moment looked like. I photograph what it felt like.”

How it works

  1. ①

    We talk, briefly.

    A call or WhatsApp voice note. You tell me the occasion, the venue, what matters most. I tell you exactly how I'd cover it.

  2. ②

    I disappear into your event.

    Quiet shoes, two bodies, no flash unless the room demands it. Your guests won't perform for the camera — that's the point.

  3. ③

    Frames, fast.

    A same-night teaser for your socials, the full edited gallery within 48 hours, delivered as a private link you can share with everyone.
